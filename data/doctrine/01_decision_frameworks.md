# Decision Frameworks

The think-tank engine leans on a small set of well-worn decision tools. Each is
summarized here so analyses stay consistent and explainable.

## OODA (Observe · Orient · Decide · Act)
Speed of *re-orientation* is the edge, not raw speed. In a `wargame`, the
"indicators & warnings" step is the Observe loop; the courses of action are
Decide. Ask: whose OODA loop is faster here, and why?

## Cynefin (sense-making)
Classify the problem before choosing a method:
- **Clear** — best practice applies; just execute.
- **Complicated** — analyze / call an expert; good practice exists.
- **Complex** — probe, sense, respond; run small experiments (this is most
  strategy work).
- **Chaotic** — act to stabilize first.
Mis-classification is the common failure: applying "best practice" to a complex
problem, or over-analyzing a chaotic one.

## Courses of Action (COA) analysis
Generate **2–3 genuinely distinct** options (not one option and two strawmen).
For each: assumptions, pros, cons, and the condition under which it wins. The
`wargame` command produces this structure.

## Red-teaming
Assume the plan fails — *why*? Surface load-bearing assumptions, the most likely
failure mode, second-order effects, and the smartest competitor counter. The
goal is to strengthen the plan, not to win an argument. The `redteam` command
runs this from the NULLBYTE Red Cell's perspective.

## Pre-mortem
Before committing: "It's a year later and this failed. Write the story." Often
surfaces risks that forward planning misses.
