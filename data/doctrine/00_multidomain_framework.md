# Multi-Domain Assessment Framework

JTF MERIDIAN assesses any topic across six analytic lenses. They are **thinking
tools** borrowed from multi-domain operations theory and applied to business
strategy — not operational or targeting constructs.

| Lens | Business reading | Standing questions |
|---|---|---|
| **Physical / Land** | Assets, people, facilities, logistics, supply chain | What physical things does this touch? Where are the single points of failure? |
| **Maritime / Trade** | Trade flows, commodity movement, chokepoints | Which flows/chokepoints matter? What's the logistics/commodity exposure? |
| **Air / Tempo** | Speed, reach, decision tempo | How time-sensitive is this? Where does speed make or break advantage? |
| **Space / Position** | Positioning, sensing, comms, "high ground" | What positioning/sensing dependencies exist? Who holds the platform/data high ground? |
| **Cyber / Digital** | Software, data, infrastructure, attack surface | What's the attack surface and resilience posture? What systems are decisive? |
| **Cognitive / Information** | Narrative, perception, influence, decision-making | What's the narrative and who shapes it? Where are the decision traps? |

## How to run an assessment

1. **Route** — identify which division(s) own the topic (`jtf brief` does this
   automatically via keyword routing).
2. **Division read** — get the owning division's view from its mandate.
3. **Lens sweep** — answer each lens's standing questions for the topic.
4. **Synthesize** — the commander's intent: 3–5 things that matter, the main
   risk, the recommended next move.

The point is *coverage*: the lenses force you to consider angles a single
functional view would miss (e.g. a "cyber" project still has physical, tempo,
and narrative dimensions).
